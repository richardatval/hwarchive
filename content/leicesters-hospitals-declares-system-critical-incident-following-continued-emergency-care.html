Leicester's Hospitals declares system critical incident following continued emergency care pressures

11/01/2017

Message from Leicester's Hospitals:

Dear friend and stakeholder,

Earlier today we made the decision to declare a System Critical Incident. This follows an extended period of pressure both in Leicester’s Hospitals and across LLR since the festive period. This is a step up in response as we have been on a critical incident since the New Year, however it has not improved our position.

Our situation worsened over the last 48 hours with patients experiencing very long waits for ward beds and unfortunately too many patients experiencing ambulance handover delays which is not the experience we would wish for our patients. Our Clinical Decisions Unit (CDU, at the Glenfield) has been extremely busy, as has our Emergency Department (at the Royal), seeing excessively high numbers of patients.

As you will have seen in the national and local media, we are not the only NHS Trust that is facing these extremely challenging pressures.  We continue to remind patients to make wise choices when they need health care advice and treatment and to seek suitable advice as soon as they begin to feel unwell and to always use alternatives to the Emergency Department wherever possible.

Other parts of the health and social care system are stepping up to support us and our staff are, as ever, doing a tremendous job in very difficult circumstances.

It would be really helpful and supportive if the following messages around accessing alternative NHS services could be shared across your networks.  These messages will also be used in our social media feeds.

We would also encourage people, particularly older people, to seek healthcare advice as soon as they begin to fell unwell.

If it is not an emergency, please: Call NHS 111:  If you need help fast but don't think it's a 999 emergency, you should call NHS 111. The service is available 24 hours a day, seven days a week, and is free to call from landlines and mobile phones.

It can signpost you to a range of local health services, including doctors, community nurses, emergency dental care and late opening chemists. The 111 service can also put you in contact with the GP out-of-hours service, which can arrange for you to see a doctor or nurse during evenings and weekends if needed.

Visit an NHS Walk-in Centre:  We have eight urgent care centres, walk-in centres and minor injuries units across Leicester, Leicestershire and Rutland that offer access to a range of treatment for minor illnesses and injuries including, lacerations, stomach upsets, burns and strains – some also have X-ray facilities. You will be seen by an experienced nurse, without an appointment.  Details of locations are here: http://www.choosebetter.org.uk/urgent-care/

Visit your GP:  GP practices are normally the first point of call for ongoing illnesses or injuries when self-care has not relieved the symptoms.

General Practitioners (doctors) can provide a wide range of family health services. Practice nurses and Health Care Assistants often work alongside the GPs to help support patients’ everyday health.

Can’t get an appointment with your GP or you need one 6pm – 8am?

Patients that are registered with any Leicester City practice can get an appointment with a GP or a prescribing nurse at one of three healthcare hubs. Patients can use the Westcotes hub from 8am to 8pm, seven days a week, or the Belgrave and Saffron hubs, which open weekdays 6.30pm to 10pm and at weekends and bank holidays 12 noon to 8pm.

Healthcare Hub addresses:

  • Westcotes Health Centre, Fosse Road South, Leicester, LE3 0LP.
  • Belgrave Health Centre, 52 Brandon Street, Leicester, LE4 6AW.
  • Saffron Health, 612 Saffron Lane, Leicester, LE2 6TD.

Appointments can be made for the same day or up to 48 hours in advance by calling 0116 366 0560 or NHS 111 from 8am to 10pm, 7 days a week. It is the same number for all the hubs.

More information on the hub opening times can be found here.

If you or your family needs urgent medical care when your GP surgery is closed, phone the usual surgery number and your call will be re-directed to the GP out-of-hours service (Monday to Friday 6.30pm-8am, Saturday, Sunday and public holidays 24 hours). 

Visit your local pharmacy:  If you need advice for minor health problems you can speak to your pharmacist for on-the-spot advice and remedies for a wide range of problems such as stomach upsets, coughs and colds, cystitis and emergency contraception. Find your local pharmacy here.

You should only call 999 or visit ED in a real emergency:  This includes a serious injury or life-threatening problem such as loss of consciousness, severe breathing difficulty, heavy bleeding, severe chest pain, suspected broken bones, deep wounds (e.g. stab wounds), swallowing something harmful or poisonous or a drug overdose.
