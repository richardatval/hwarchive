Have your say in upcoming inspection of local mental health services

15/09/2016

The Care Quality Commission (CQC) will shortly inspect the Mental Health services provided by Leicestershire Partnership NHS Trust.

We’re working to ensure your voice is heard in the inspection. We want to hear the views of patients, carers and their families across Leicestershire.  

If you have something to say about the Mental Health services you have received, please complete our survey.

About the CQC

The Care Quality Commission (CQC) is the independent regulator for health and social care in England. They make sure services such as hospitals, care homes, dentists and GP surgeries provide people with safe, effective, compassionate and high-quality care and encourage these services to improve. 

CQC inspections seek to answer five key questions about each service they inspect:

  • Is it safe?
  • Is it effective?
  • Is it caring?
  • Is it responsive to people’s needs?
  • Is it well-led?

Your views will be essential to the CQC’s ability to answer these questions in its upcoming inspection of Leicestershire Partnership NHS Trust’s Mental Health services, so we are urging all local residents to speak out.

What services do you want to hear about?

The CQC will be inspecting the following services delivered by LPT:

  • Acute wards for adults of working age and psychiatric intensive care units
  • Child and adolescent mental health wards
  • Community mental health services learning disabilities or autism and for older people
  • Community services for adults
  • Forensic inpatient/secure wards
  • Long stay/rehabilitation mental health wards for working age adults
  • Mental health crisis services and health-based places of safety
  • Specialist community mental health services for children and young people
  • Wards for older people with mental health problems and for people with learning disabilities or autism
  • Community health services for adults, children, young people and families
  • Community inpatient services
  • End of life care 

If you have had experiences with any of these services, we want to hear from you.
