Campaign to save the East Midlands Congenital Heart Centre

28/07/2016

Healthwatch Leicestershire, Healthwatch Leicester and the Leicester Mercury Patients’ Panel are supporting patients and public who are shocked at the NHSE announcement that it is minded not to support EMCHC as a centre where children’s heart surgery will take place in future.

There is anger that NHSE can be so hypocritical in stressing all Health and Social Care agencies have public and patient involvement and then pay no heed to what they value and what’s working well for them.

This is what parents and members of the public have told us:

“I cannot understand why, when there has been recent outcry about the distance parents have to travel to be with unwell children, NHS England have chosen to make this worse for people in Leicester.” 

“Leicester is a poor and deprived city with 50% of the city from other cultures, we depend on local and culturally appropriate services with consultants, doctors and nurses who we know understand the context they are working in”

Karen Chouhan Chair of Healthwatch Leicester City said:

“Healthwatch cannot stand by and let this happen, this stated intention from NHSE betrays the evidence of meeting all the standards set by them for the congenital heart centre, as John Adler (Chief Executive UHL) has indicated.  He also said closure would mean decades of experience lost, innovation lost and knowledge lost.

Why then has NHSE had made this decision 3 months into a 5 year plan to establish full compliance? Why do senior people in NHS England make plans based on clear quality indicators and then retract them when work and investment has already been agreed and started?

Leicester City Football Club – has just donated £2m to the Children’s hospital, £1m has just been invested according to UHL into increasing bed bases including in an adolescent unit and a well equipped short stay bay. In terms of quality, patient satisfaction and financially this decision does not stack up.”

The Leicester Mercury Patients’ Panel have also pointed out that when NHS England established the New Congenital Heart Disease Review it had the following aims which are seemingly being met by the EMCHC:

  • Securing the best outcomes for all patients – not just lowest mortality but reduced disability and an improved opportunity for survivors to lead better lives;
  • Tackling variation – ensuring that services across the country consistently meet national standards, and are able to offer resilient 24/7 care, and;
  • Improving patient experience – including how information is provided to patients and their families, and consideration of access and support for families when they have to be away from home.

Sally Ruane, chairwoman of the Leicester Mercury Patients’ Panel said:

“It appears to me that the basis for closure can be questioned on several levels as UHL have asserted they are meeting the aims of the review as above and are only 3 months into the 5 year trajectory to full compliance. If outcomes are as good as has been suggested e.g.

  • Cancellation rates are better than the national average, and friends and family test results have increased to 99%.
  • UHL expect to meet the standard of an average of 375 cases per year with 3 Surgeons over the next 3 Years. Currently the surgeons are performing 332 between them (in 2015/16) and this is well above any evidence based quality threshold.

The reason for the failure therefore remains a bit of a mystery and it could mean that, if it goes ahead, closure would not improve quality or reduce variation but would negatively impact patients and family experience if they have to travel further to unfamiliar locations.”

Dr Ruane added, “It is essential that a formal public consultation takes place so that all the facts can be brought into the public arena and so that the full ramifications of closure can be assessed”.

Rick Moore Chair of Healthwatch Leicestershire said:

“Healthwatch is shocked at the decision by NHS England to shut down the Children’s Heart Service Unit at Glenfield Hospital.

In previous public consultation on this matter (December 2014), a major concern for residents in Leicester and Leicestershire was the fate of national ECMO  (mechanical help with breathing) services, based at Glenfield, should Children’s Heart Disease services be lost within UHL.

A commissioning model that best suits the needs of the population is vital and NHS England Commissioners need to work with UHL in a co-commissioning model to join up commissioning not just for paediatric cardiac services but also that for congenital heart disease, fetal medicine, paediatric surgery and other related specialist services.

We strongly oppose the decision and have concerns about the impact this could have for patients, parents and carers in Leicestershire” 

We call on NHSE to reconsider urgently – it is important that the children and families who use these services and that people across Leicestershire are given their say and we support the local campaign to save the unit and related services.

Further press releases will follow as momentum increases on this critically important issue.
