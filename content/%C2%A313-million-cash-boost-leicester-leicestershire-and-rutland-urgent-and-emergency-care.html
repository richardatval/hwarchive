£1.3 million cash boost for Leicester, Leicestershire and Rutland Urgent and Emergency Care Network

28/01/2016

£1.3 million of funding has been allocated by NHS England to help develop new ways of delivering urgent and emergency care in Leicester, Leicestershire and Rutland.

This money has been given to the Leicester, Leicestershire and Rutland System Resilience Group, comprised of health and social care organisations working across the area. In July, this group was selected by NHS England as a “vanguard” for Urgent and Emergency Care.

This area is one of eight vanguards who will be taking the lead nationally in developing new models of urgent and emergency care, which will then be used as blueprint for the NHS moving forward and the inspiration for the rest of the health system.

The money covers the year 2015/16, and vanguards are also receiving practical assistance as part of a package of national support announced in the summer to enable them to make the changes they want at pace.

In Leicester, Leicestershire and Rutland the funding will be used to help with the ongoing development of projects such as the new front door streaming service at Leicester Royal Infirmary, seven day working initiatives and other services.

Toby Sanders, chair of the LLR System Resilience Group said “We welcome this financial support, which will to help us achieve our vision of creating an urgent and emergency care network which simplifies things for patients and provides them with the right care, first time.

“We hear from patients and carers through our engagement work, that they find our current services fragmented and often aren’t aware of the options available to them, or how to choose between them. Through the work of the vanguard we will streamline the care they receive and help them to access it when they need it without having to worry about how to navigate through complex health and social care systems.”

Plans of the Urgent and Emergency Care include bringing together health and social care services from across the area as one network, including ambulance services, NHS 111 and out of hours care, as well as community and social care services. Patients will be able to access these services through a single point of contact which will provide clinical triage, advice and signposting and reduce the sometimes complex ways in which patients currently need to access care. Urgent care services in the community will also come together, to provide care and support in the community 24 hours a day, seven days and work together to help patients to avoid hospital attendance.

Mental health services will also be developed to better meet the needs of patients and have the facilities and staff in place who can treat any mental health issues with the same priority as physical ones. Improvements will be made at the Leicester Royal Infirmary, redesigning access to urgent and emergency care and sending patients to the place which can provide the most suitable care for them, work that is being piloted currently through the “Front Door” project.

Samantha Jones, Director – New Care Models Programme, said: “We are pleased to be able to support the Leicester, Leicestershire and Rutland Urgent and Emergency Care Network with both financial and practical support.  The changes they have planned will help deliver a raft of benefits for patients, local people and staff.  We are also supporting them to help spread good practice across the wider NHS and social care community.”
