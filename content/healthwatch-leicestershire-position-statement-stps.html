Healthwatch Leicestershire Position Statement on STPs

31/03/2017

Sustainability and Transformation Plans (STPs) are place based, multi-year plans built around the health and social care needs of the local population. They were announced in the NHS planning guidance published in December 2015. Our HWL ‘footprint’ covers Leicester, Leicestershire and Rutland. Leaders from health and care organisations across the footprint must work together to transform the way services are delivered to better meet the needs of the local population and to ensure that they are sustainable for the future. STPs must:

  • Improve quality and developing new models of care  
  • Improve health and wellbeing  
  • Improve the efficiency of services  

Will local people be involved in these plans?  

Guidance says that to succeed, STPs will need to be developed with, and based upon, the needs of local patients and communities:

‘Involving people, communities and stakeholders meaningfully is essential to effective service improvement and transformation, from collectively identifying problems and designing solutions to influencing delivery and review. Effective communication and involvement throughout the process will help to build ownership and support for proposals to transform health and care and will help identify potential area of concern’[1]         

What is the role of local Healthwatch Leicestershire?

Healthwatch Leicestershire was set up in 2013 to encourage local people to speak up about their experiences and views of health and care. We exist to:  

  • Promote the involvement of local people in health and social care  
  • Monitor the quality of health and social care services  
  • Provide information about health and social care  
  • Using the voice of local people, influence the decision makers of health and social care.  

What is the role of Healthwatch Leicestershire and the STP?

Healthwatch is here to help make health and social care better for people that use services. We believe that the best way to do this is by designing local services around their needs and experiences.

As the local Healthwatch for the County, everything we say and do is informed by our connections to local people and our expertise is grounded in their experience. We are the only body looking solely at people’s experience across all health and social care services. Healthwatch gives people a powerful voice locally and nationally using patient experience to improve services today or helping shape them for tomorrow.

Healthwatch Leicestershire is all about local voices being able to influence the delivery and design of local services. Not just people who use them, but anyone who might need to in future.

HWL Do’s and Don’ts

  1. We will not pass judgment on the STP, but will challenge to ensure that Leicestershire residents have their say in how services are run in the future by acting as a critical friend.  
  2. We will work to ensure that there is a strong voice for local people in the process and that this is reflected in the decisions will be taken.  
  3. Our role is to scrutinize and advise the organisations involved in the STP on effective communications, engagement, and consultation with local people.  
  4. We will monitor the quality of the communications, engagement and consultation.  
  5. We will challenge the STP process if we have any concerns about these areas.  
  6. We will attend locality and stakeholder meetings with the public as observers to listen to the views of local people where possible.  
  7. We will inform, promote and publicise to Healthwatch members and the public about events and activities relating to the STP using our communications channels.  
  8. We will engage at the strategic level on Programme Boards relating to transforming services to provide independent scrutiny and assurance from a public and patient perspective taking into account patient experience (insights, choices and points of views) for the design and delivery of commissioned services.

What does Healthwatch Leicestershire want to see?

Healthwatch Leicestershire will be looking for evidence of:

  • Good quality and accessible information for local people about STP as it develops  
  • Opportunities for the voluntary sector to be involved  
  • Engagement with patients and organisations which can represent patients and service users  
  • Co-producing any new services with the people who use them  
  • Formal consultation should it be required  

We want to see the involvement of people at all 3 stages:  

Inform: People need good quality information so that they can develop their thinking and views on the subject including the options.  

Engage: Open conversations between the public and the people who plan and deliver health and care services. There needs to be opportunities for people to get involved whether that is through a local meeting or through workshops to co-produce care pathways.  

Consult: Consultation is a formal process that seeks the view of the public on what is being proposed before a final decision is taken.  

Engagement stage

We will want to see the impact assessment for service changes and where did it clearly identified communities that should be involved in pre -consultation engagement?  

We will seek assurance that the most appropriate method of engagement is used for each identified group.  

We will want to be assured that there was sufficient time at the engagement stage to develop a robust set of options and that these are then taken forward for formal consultation.  

Public consultations: what can you expect?  

Consultation is best carried out after having informed and involved people in the ‘thinking’ stages beforehand.

All organisations must adhere to legal guidance which is known as the Gunning Principles:  

  1. Consultation must take place when the proposal is still at a formative stage  
  2. The reasons for the change must be clearly stated so that the public can understand and consider the proposals  
  3. Adequate time must be given for the public to consider and respond  
  4. The organisation must be able to evidence that they have listened and considered the feedback they receive from the public.  

If there is any legal challenge to any consultation process, the above principles are used to review the process.

Want to know more?

We encourage feedback about health and social care services so that we can influence change and where appropriate bring challenge based on what local residents tell us.
