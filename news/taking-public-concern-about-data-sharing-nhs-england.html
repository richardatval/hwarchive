Taking public concern about data sharing to NHS England

26/02/2014

According to recent media reports people are still largely in the dark about plans to share their personal medical records through NHS Care.Data, despite a nationwide mail-out campaign.

To find out more about how and why your personal information may be used with the introductions of Care.Data see NHS England's Better information means better care leaflet.

A dedicated information line is available for patients to call to find out about how the NHS plan to use patients medical information on 0300 456 3531. The line is open Monday to Friday, 8am to 8pm and on Saturdays from 9am to 3pm.

A text phone service is also available on 0208 7428620 or you can email the enquiries email to england.cdoqueries@nhs.net

Anna Bradley, Chair of Healthwatch England, said:

“Crucially they (NHS England) have agreed to Healthwatch England’s request to see the roll-out of care.data delayed to allow more time to ensure the public are fully informed. Over the coming months the Healthwatch network will continue to play a key role listening to the concerns of local communities, helping to inform them about what’s happening and working with NHS England to improve their communications with the public so each of us can make an informed decision.”

Having listened carefully to the concerns of GP’s, Patients and others, NHS England accept that the public need more time to learn about how patient information will be shared and have now extended the care.data public awareness campaign until the Autumn:

  • NHS England will begin collecting data from GP surgeries in the Autumn, instead of the spring to allow more time to build understanding of the benefits of using the information, what safeguards are in place, and how people can object if they choose to;
  • NHS England will work with patients and professional groups – including the BMA, RCGP, Healthwatch and listen to GPs – to develop additional practical steps to promote awareness with patients and the public, and ensure information is accessible and reaches all sections of the community, including people with disabilities;
  • NHS England will look into further measures that could be taken to build public confidence, in particular steps relating to scrutiny of ways in which the information will be used to benefit NHS patients;
