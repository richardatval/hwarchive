Patients told it’s good to share

26/02/2014

From 1 March 2014 NHS patients in Leicester, Leicestershire and Rutland are to be asked for permission to share their medical records when they are referred from one NHS service to another, to enable faster, more connected treatment.

When patients receive healthcare or medical treatment, information about them and the care they have received is often recorded on a computer system called SystmOne. Sometimes it is necessary to share this information with other healthcare services that also need to treat them, such as community nurses, the GP Out of Hours service and NHS 111. Currently this information can only be shared with other healthcare organisations by letter, email, fax or phone. At times this can slow down the patient’s treatment and means information about them is hard to access.

As a result the three local clinical commissioning groups (CCGs) responsible for planning and buying healthcare in the city and county are enabling primary care providers like GPs to share medical information held on the SystmOne computer system with community care providers such as urgent care centres and district nurses. The project, known as the Enhanced Data Sharing Model (EDSM), is set to go live in general practices that use SystmOne* in Leicester, Leicestershire and Rutland on March 1, 2014.

Not all GP practices in Leicester, Leicestershire and Rutland use SystmOne, which means not every patient will be affected by EDSM.

Patients can decide whether they want to share their full medical record with other NHS services they use, or whether they wish to opt out. There is no need for patients to take any action now. Next time they visit a healthcare provider or they register with a new GP practice that uses SystmOne, they will be asked for permission for their data to be shared.

Even if patients want most of their data to be shared, they will be able to have certain parts of their record marked private by talking to their GP. Equally, patients will have the right to give consent to some providers but not others.

Dr Tony Bentley, Leicester City Clinical Commissioning Board GP and clinical lead for EDSM, said: “Health services can provide much better care by sharing the medical information they hold about patients. But they can’t do this without the patient’s permission.

“That consent allows the record to be shared so we can provide care as quickly and effectively as possible.

“Delays at other healthcare providers will be minimised if patient records are immediately available. This will allow quicker and safer patient care.

“The Enhanced Data Sharing Model is another example of the NHS working smarter to improve the patient experience. It is recommended that practices make the data available to be shared, but that sharing will only happen with the patient’s explicit consent, apart from in very rare life threatening situations. ”

If patients would like to give permission for their data to be shared or they would like to speak to someone about whether their practice uses SystmOne, they should contact their GP practice.
