Find out about mental health services

21/09/2015

It is estimated that one in four people in the UK will suffer from an instance of poor mental health or depression over the course of a single year[1].

To help tackle this issue West Leicestershire Clinical Commissioning Group (CCG) is working with patients to ensure that mental health services better meet the needs of local people and support their recovery. This is part of the Better Care Together programme, a five-year plan to transform health and social care services in Leicester, Leicestershire and Rutland.

Andy Murtha has been diagnosed with bi-polar disorder and is now using his experiences with his care and treatment to help others and improve services. He is a champion of patient care and is working closely with West Leicestershire CCG.

”I crashed into severe depression and anxieties.” said Andy. “I went into what used to be called the nervous breakdown, curled up into a ball and took a year and a half to two years to come out of the ball. I then spent months to get out on to the street as I had developed agoraphobia and social phobia. I was suicidal and I used to rip my skin, and inside of me there were no real feelings whatsoever. Because of mood swings in my life I then developed a form of bi-polar disorder which means I go into depression and into highs.

“The NHS were very good in stopping my condition from getting worse and importantly they were there when I needed them, but in those days it was very medical and there was no recovery trail. So when I got a little better there was nowhere else to go.

“Luckily for me, I met somebody who carried on my recovery. He had been through the same experiences as me and was my peer support. It was through his advice that I took up photography as a hobby. The NHS were there at the beginning and eventually I found somebody who could help me and this is the reason I feel so strongly that people benefit from peer support.

“I got well enough to speak out for people that are my peer group. If we can get everyone working together then we can change the system which can improve the focus on recovery with peer support and signposting to support services. If we can get people to a position to where they enjoy life in some way that would be my ultimate goal.”

Dr Peter Cannon, a working GP in Loughborough and Clinical Lead for Mental Health Services at West Leicestershire CCG, said: “Transforming mental health care is a key priority for us as the number of people with mental health problems continues to rise.  We are working closely with Andy and other patients to look at how we can provide easily accessible, person-centred care.

“We can only transform care if we change how we think about it, and move from a system where we only treat the condition to one where we work with patients to help them to not only recover, but give them the resilience to remain well. It becomes a wellness model rather than an illness model. We’re working in partnership with local voluntary and community assets to help patients to sustain themselves long term. Part of that will be through services such as peer support and social prescribing which will help to link people up to activities in the community where they will benefit from non-medical sources of support.”

These changes will be discussed in more detail in one of four fast paced interactive workshops which are taking place on 30 September at the Ramada Hotel in Loughborough. West Leicestershire CCG is organising a health market place and conference to inform attendees about local healthcare services and offer members of the public a chance to talk directly to local health and social care leaders about the CCG’s approach to four key areas of healthcare, including mental health. This event will also incorporate their Annual General Meeting.

Dr Cannon added: “People can find out more about the changes we are making to mental health services, and hear Andy’s story, at one of the interactive workshops which we are holding after our annual general meeting on 30 September at the Ramada Hotel in Loughborough. We hope as many people as possible will be able to join us to hear more about the work we do for the residents of West Leicestershire.”

The event starts at 5pm for the marketplace and 6pm for the AGM and conference. Everyone is welcome and people can register at www.westleicestershireccg.nhs.uk/agm2015. Alternatively they can email: communications@westleicestershireccg.nhs.uk or telephone 0116 295 4183.
